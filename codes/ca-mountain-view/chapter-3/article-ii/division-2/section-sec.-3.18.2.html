SEC. 3.18.2. - Prohibited signs adjacent to freeways—Exhibiting advertising to freeway users.

No sign shall be painted, attached, installed, erected, constructed or relocated in the city which is visible from a freeway if such sign is designed to have or has advertising thereupon maintained primarily to be viewed from the freeway. When any sign is intended to be located within six hundred (600) feet from the edge of the right-of-way of the designated freeway so that the advertising thereon is visible from such freeway, it shall be deemed prima facie to be maintained primarily to be viewed therefrom.

(Ord. 175.622, 8/8/60.)
